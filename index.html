Phase 0 Purpose and Governing Rules

Phase 0 is a 2-week diagnostic and re-entry block. Its job is not to make you tired. Its job is to rebuild tissue tolerance, re-establish aerobic rhythm, groove movement quality, and generate the data needed to program Phase 1 correctly.

THE ONE RULE. Every session ends with you feeling you could have done meaningfully more. This is the success criterion for every single workout in these two weeks. If you're grinding, you're doing it wrong.
Loads are anchored to current capacity, not pre-mono maxes. Bench works at ~155–170 lbs. Squat works at ~175–195 lbs. These are not light — they are right. You are reloading a known pattern, not testing limits. Resist the urge to push heavier.
All work sets are 3–4 RIR (reps in reserve). If you reach 3 RIR on any set, stop. Do not squeeze out extra reps. Do not ego-load. RIR is the intensity dial — use it.
Time cap is hard at 70 minutes. If a section is running long, cut the last accessory, not the main lift. Never sacrifice the primary movement to fit in extras. Extras are earned — the main lift is mandatory.
Knee is a yellow flag throughout. Any increase in knee ache during a set is a cue to modify, not push through. See the Adjustment Rules tab for exact protocols.
No running this phase. Conditioning is bike and swim only. This is not a compromise — it is the correct prescription for a post-mono athlete with a yellow-flag knee building an aerobic base.

How to Read the Session Tables

ℹ
Sets × Reps are working sets only, not warm-up sets. Before your first exercise each session, do 1–2 ramp-up sets at 50% and 70% of working weight. These take 2–3 minutes and do not count toward the working sets. RIR = reps in reserve. A 3 RIR set of 10 means you stop at 10 when you could have done 13. Rest is clock rest between working sets. Use it. Don't rush.
⏱
Estimated times per section are realistic, not optimistic. They assume you start sets on time. If a section runs over, trim the last set of the last exercise — never cut the primary movement or its rest periods.
WEEK 1 — Establish
🎯
Week 1 objective: Find your honest working loads. Every set should finish with at least 3 reps clearly left in the tank. Log the weight, how it moved, and how your knee felt. This data writes your Week 2 and then your Phase 1.
MON
Lower Body
Quad · Hinge · Knee Durability
⏱ Target: ~65 min total including warm-up

Warm-Up — 10 min

  • 5 min easy stationary bike — light resistance, get blood moving. Per JN: core temp up before any dynamic work, especially critical for morning sessions.
  • Foam roll — quads, IT band, calves, glutes — 60 sec each side. Brief and targeted.
  • Front/back leg swings × 12 each leg
  • Side/side leg swings × 12 each leg
  • Bodyweight squat × 10 — slow, deep as knee permits, feel for tracking
  • Hip flexor stretch — lunge position, 30 sec each side
  • Terminal knee extensions with band (or bodyweight) × 15 each leg — loop a band around a rack at knee height, step back, slight bend, squeeze into extension. This directly fires the VMO and is your best pre-activation for a tweaky knee.
Warm-up est: 10 min
A1 — Primary Lift (~18 min)
ExerciseSets × RepsRIRRestNotes
Goblet Squat 3 × 8 3–4 2 min Start here, not barbell. Use a 50–65 lb dumbbell. 3-sec controlled descent, pause at bottom 1 sec. Knees tracking over toes. This is intentionally not the barbell back squat — it unloads the spine and teaches the pattern clean. Upgrade to barbell squat in Week 2 if this is effortless and knee is silent.
⚠ KNEE SUBSTITUTION: If goblet squat produces knee ache during — not just pre-session stiffness — switch to leg press at a high foot position (feet high on plate reduces knee flexion demand). Same sets/reps/RIR.
A2 — Hip Hinge (~15 min)
ExerciseSets × RepsRIRRestNotes
Romanian Deadlift (barbell) 3 × 10 3–4 2 min Start at ~135 lbs. Hips back, slight knee bend, bar drags down your legs, neutral lower back throughout. This is not a conventional deadlift — no pull from the floor. Stop when you feel hamstring tension, not when the bar hits the floor. Almost zero knee compressive load here — it's your safest lower-body strength work this phase.
B — Unilateral / Knee Durability (~15 min)
ExerciseSets × RepsRIRRestNotes
Dumbbell Step-Up (low box, ~10–12") 3 × 8 each leg 3–4 90 sec Use 20–30 lb dumbbells. Drive through the heel of the top leg. Do NOT bounce off the bottom leg. Step-ups at a low box = controlled unilateral load with minimal knee angle stress. Drawn from the JSOC single-leg program as a foundational unilateral pattern.
Single-Leg Glute Bridge (bodyweight) 2 × 12 each leg 3 60 sec Floor, one heel planted, drive hip up, squeeze glute at top for 1 sec. Builds posterior chain support around the knee without any knee-compressive load. From JSOC asymmetrical program correctives list.
⚠ KNEE SUBSTITUTION (Step-Up): If step-up produces ache during the movement, reduce box height to a 6" step or substitute Split Squat (bodyweight only, short stance). Do not add load until the movement is clean and quiet.
C — Knee Durability Finisher (~5 min)
ExerciseSets × RepsRIRRestNotes
Terminal Knee Extension (band) 2 × 15 each leg N/A 45 sec Band looped around rack at mid-shin height. Face rack, step back until band is taut at bend. Straighten knee fully against the band — no hip movement. This is a rehab/prehab finisher, not a strength move. Slow and deliberate. VMO activation and tendon loading at low stress.
Working sets: 11
Main lift sets: 3
Est. total: ~63 min
TUE
Conditioning — Bike + Upper Durability
Zone 2 · Aerobic Base · Shoulder Prehab
⏱ Target: ~45 min total

Bike — Zone 2 Steady State

Duration: 25–28 minutes. Intensity: RPE 4–5/10.

Talk test (your only gauge since you have no HR monitor): You should be able to speak in complete sentences without pausing for breath. If you can only manage a few words, slow down. This is Zone 2 — not a workout, an aerobic stimulus.

Set resistance at a light-moderate level. Cadence 75–90 RPM feels natural for Zone 2. This should feel almost too easy. That's correct.

If fatigued: Drop to 20 min. Keep RPE the same — do not compensate with harder effort to make a shorter session "worth it."

Upper Durability Finisher — 10–12 min
ExerciseSets × RepsRestNotes
Band Pull-Apart 3 × 15 45 sec Light band. Arms straight, pull apart to T position, squeeze shoulder blades for 1 sec at end range. This is rear delt and scapular retractor work — directly from JN's warm-up and shoulder durability protocols.
Cable or Band External Rotation 2 × 15 each side 45 sec Elbow at 90°, pinned to your side. Rotate forearm out slowly. Light resistance, feel the infraspinatus working. Per JN warm-up protocol — cable external rotation is listed directly.
Prone Trap Raise (incline bench or floor) 2 × 12 45 sec Lie face down on incline bench. Arms at Y-angle, thumbs up, lift until level with torso — no higher. Squeeze mid-back. Per JN's warm-up protocol. Mind-muscle connection with the mid back.
Bike: 25–28 min
Finisher: ~12 min
Est. total: ~42 min

Upper Body
Press · Pull · Shoulder Health

Warm-Up — 8 min

  • Band pull-aparts × 15 — scapular activation
  • Front/back leg swings × 10 each — general lower looseness
  • Cable external rotation × 12 each arm — light, rotator cuff on

A1 — Primary Press (~18 min)
ExerciseSets × RepsRIRRestNotes
Barbell Flat Bench Press 3 × 8 3–4 2.5 min Working at ~155–165 lbs. Per JN's bench technique: elbows at 45°, scapulae retracted and depressed, stay firm on bench. This should feel controlled, not labored. 3–4 RIR means you stop at 8 when 12 is technically possible. DO NOT max out.
⚠ BENCH SUBSTITUTION (per JN): If barbell bench is unavailable — Dumbbell Bench Press or Hammer Strength Machine Press. Same sets/reps/RIR.
A2 — Primary Pull (~15 min)
ExerciseSets × RepsRIRRestNotes
Seated Cable Row (neutral grip) 3 × 10 3–4 2 min Moderate weight. Retract scapulae during the pull, protract on the return — don't just pull with your arms. Row to your sternum, not your belly button. Keep the torso upright. 2-sec return.
Lat Pulldown (shoulder-width grip) 3 × 10 3–4 90 sec Per JN: elbows straight out to sides, 1.5× shoulder-width grip. Pull to upper chest. Don't lean back excessively. Mind-muscle: think "elbows to back pockets."
B — Shoulder & Accessory (~14 min)
ExerciseSets × RepsRIRRestNotes
Dumbbell Lateral Raise 3 × 12 3 75 sec Light dumbbells — 15–25 lbs. Slight forward lean, pinky slightly higher than thumb. Slow negative. Per JN: this builds the medial delt which is key for shoulder width. Prioritized because it matters for your physique goal.
Dumbbell Curl (supinated) 2 × 12 3 60 sec Drive the pinky harder into the handle than the pointer finger. Per JN's supinated curl cue. Light, controlled. Arm work starts here to begin the hypertrophy signal in a time-efficient way.
Rope Tricep Extension 2 × 12 3 60 sec Per JN: pull arm behind torso at bottom, keep upper arm stationary. Elbow work as an easy Phase 0 accessory to begin tissue loading.
Working sets: 16
Est. total: ~64 min
THU
Conditioning — Swim or Bike + Core
Zone 2 · Aerobic Base · Core Stability
⏱ Target: ~45 min total

Option A: Swimming (preferred if accessible)

Duration: 25–28 min easy swimming. Mix strokes freely — crawl, backstroke, breaststroke. Intensity: Easy, never breathless. This is the best Zone 2 modality in your toolkit: zero knee load, gentle full-body, deeply aerobic and excellent for post-mono recovery.

Do not worry about pace, distance, or efficiency. The goal is continuous movement at low intensity. Rest at the wall briefly if needed — not a problem in Week 1.

Option B: Bike (if no swim access)

Duration: 28 min Zone 2 bike, same talk-test standard as Tuesday. Slightly longer than Tuesday since there's no shoulder finisher to follow.

If fatigued: 20 min is fine. Honest easy effort beats a short hard effort every time this phase.

Core Finisher — 8 min
ExerciseSets × Reps/TimeRestNotes
Dead Bug 3 × 8 each side 45 sec Flat back pressed to floor throughout. Opposite arm and leg extend slowly — back must not arch. Anti-extension core stability. Zero spinal compression, zero knee load.
Plank (forearm) 2 × 25 sec 45 sec Squeeze glutes, keep hips level. Per JN's lower body protocol: quality over duration. 25 seconds of honest tension beats 60 seconds of sagging hips.
Pallof Press (cable or band) 2 × 10 each side 45 sec Anti-rotation. Anchor band at chest height. Press out, hold 2 sec, return. From JSOC asymmetrical program — this is a foundational trunk stability move for tactical athletes.
Conditioning: 25–28 min
Core: ~8 min
Est. total: ~40 min

Daily Adjustment Rules

These are your in-session decision rules. They override the written prescription when the conditions below are met. Read them once before the program starts so you know them by instinct in the gym.

🦵 Knee Pain — Before Session
If: Knee is noticeably achy walking in, stiff, or felt bad yesterday
Do: Do the warm-up fully, including the TKE band work. Give the knee 10 min to loosen up before deciding.
If clears: Proceed with planned session. Monitor closely — if it returns, apply in-session rule below.
If does not clear: On a lower day — skip squats and step-ups. Do RDL and non-loading knee alternatives (leg press high-foot, GHR, upper body only). On an upper/conditioning day — proceed as written.
Never: Push through a knee that is actively unhappy before you even start. That is a red flag, not a green one.
🦵 Knee Pain — During Session
Mild ache warms up: Ache present on first 1–2 sets, fades by set 3 — monitor and continue with current exercise.
Ache builds across sets: Stop the exercise. Substitute: leg press (high foot) or RDL variation. Log the exercise that provoked it.
Sharp or stabbing: Stop immediately. Finish the session on upper body and non-loading exercises. No exceptions.
Swelling or instability: End the session. Rest the next lower-body day. If persists 48h, see a professional before continuing lower-body loading.
Ache lingers next day: Log it. If it happens twice on the same exercise, that exercise is flagged — report it at the Phase 1 check-in.
😴 Bad Sleep — Under ~5 hrs
Rule: Still show up. Consistency matters more than perfection. But reduce volume ~30–35%.
Lifting days: Cut last 1–2 accessories. Main lifts stay. Drop load 5–10% if bar feels heavy.
Conditioning days: Reduce duration by 25%. Keep RPE identical — do not compensate with harder effort.
Two bad nights in a row: Consider swapping a lifting day for a conditioning day, or take the day as an active rest (15–20 min easy walk).
Never: Push volume or intensity to "make up for" bad sleep. That's how injuries and setbacks happen.
⚡ General Flatness / Low Energy
Rule: Auto-regulate by RIR. If the bar feels heavier than usual, the weight is wrong for today — not you.
Drop load until working weight sits at the prescribed RIR. A lighter session done properly always beats a prescribed session done poorly.
Signs it's okay: 1–2 exercises feel flat but then you warm into the session. This is normal.
Signs to cut short: Still flat after the first two exercises with reduced load. In that case: do your primary lift only, skip accessories, leave.
🏥 Post-Mono Fatigue — The Critical One
Normal training fatigue: Tired during session, recovered within a few hours or by next morning. This is expected — it's adaptation.
Post-mono fatigue signal: Disproportionate tiredness — wiped out by a session that shouldn't have done that, or dragging for 2+ days after a session that was within the Phase 0 prescription.
If you see it: Take 2–3 fully easy or off days. Then re-enter at the previous week's volume — not where you left off. Do not try to make up the missed sessions.
If it keeps recurring: See your doctor before continuing the ramp. Post-mono fatigue relapses are real and cost far more than the sessions you'd "lose" by pausing.
The key diagnostic: You should feel at worst 80% recovered going into each session. If you consistently feel less than that after full nights of sleep, the volume is too high. Back off until you do.
